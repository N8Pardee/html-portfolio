About Me

I am a 30 year old stay-at-home dad. I homeschool my two children and we travel alongside my wife as she does travel nursing. I graduated in Spring 2024 with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science from the University of Southern Mississippi. To be frank, I have not utilized my degree at all since graduating. I switched to the stay-at-home parent role as my primary focus during school. Since graduating, I have continued learning and practicing to keep my programming skills from becoming useless. Just not to the exent as I had hoped for.

I have not completed or accomplished as many projects as I had hoped or wanted to since graduating. Partly because I cannot give my children the attention required of me as their teacher. I have set being their teacher as my primary role for now. Yet, in being honest with myself, I have found that I do not know where my path leads as a developer, when the time comes, and have felt lost since graduating school. I have a tendency to want to learn everything all at once. Computer Science is such a broad field, that I feel like a child in a toy store. I like to be knowledgable in different subtopics. I want to be like a swiss army knife, a jack of all trades, but master of none.This is probably my biggest downfall as a person trying to find work because it causes me to spin my wheels when trying to become more proficient in a certain niche. This hinderance is also my main driving force that keeps me engaged. I want to learn as much as I can because I genuinely find joy in learning and improving my skills. I'm a firm believer that if you are not learning, then you are not growing. If you do not know how to do something then figure it out, learn about it, solve the problem. My biggest strength is my desire to know more. I don't like calling in functions from code libraries without knowing how they work. I do what needs to be done when given a timeframe. But I can promise that I will look it up after all is said and done.

This about me page has turned into a long winded journal entry. I could write more about who I am, and maybe I will. But for now I feel that what I have written says enough. As far as prospective employers viewing this website goes, then I want them to see my authenticity. I am not special. I am not some super star programmer. But I will work as hard as I can to mold myself into the missing piece of their puzzle. The problem is, most employers are looking for a piece that doesn't need to be molded and I accept that.
